Twitch.tv

This is one of my favorite sites because it created a subculture of gamers that I think is really interesting. It provides entertainment and strategy from the game(s) that I love.
The eye is drawn to the middle of the screen's media player. It is the main function of the site and should be the center of attention.
I would describe it as functional, fullscreen, sidebars, centralized, and relatable.
The different channels are ranked by viewership so the popular content is really easy to find from the homepage.
It is easy to browse through.
Sometimes I am convinced that it is something I want to do because I enjoy it and sometimes I feel like my time is being wasted. It is relaxing though.
The site sells ad space to companies that I have no product for. The site hosts streamers who can sell subscriptions to their channel which offer certain ammenities. I have bought nothing from the site.

Project Euler

It taught me to enjoy learning on my own again. It was a lesosn I try to relearn every day. The table spanning the center of the site. It is not impressive based on visual appeal or organization. Instead, The site is a field of rabbit holes to explore accross the web. It requires a coding enviornment to be set up independantly but challenges you to learn math and programming. My five words are simple, barely interactive, informational, and confusing. It is easy to navigate and the site doesn't sell anything. I feel confused if I spend too much time on it. If I am on it for too long, it means I don't understand the question. Ha!
